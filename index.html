Bridging the Gap: Health, Hope, and Dignity for Every Woman.

The Aisha Health and Wellness Initiative (AHWI) delivers crucial maternal, adolescent, and preventative health services directly to girls and women in the Ogun Waterside Local Government Area of Ogun State, Nigeria. We believe health access is a fundamental right.

Our Story, Our Mission, Our People

We are a grassroots initiative founded by dedicated local residents and Nigerian diaspora professionals committed to reversing poor health trends in our region.

The Challenge We Address

In Ogun Waterside, women face significant barriers to care: long travel distances to clinics, high poverty rates, and lack of culturally sensitive health information. This results in preventable deaths and high rates of adolescent pregnancy.

Community-Led Approach

Our success is built on deep community engagement. We train local women as health ambassadors to ensure our programs are trustworthy, relevant, and sustainable from within the Waterside communities themselves.

Our Goals

By 2025, we aim to reduce maternal mortality rates in our target area by 25% and increase the use of modern contraceptives among adolescents by 40% through accessible education and free supplies.

Impact in Action

Our Three Pillars of Impact

Each program is a direct response to a community need, focusing on education, accessibility, and local empowerment.

Safe Motherhood

Safe Motherhood Initiative

We provide free education on danger signs in pregnancy and host monthly community clinics. A core focus is the Emergency Transport Voucher Scheme, ensuring rural pregnant women can reach the nearest General Hospital for delivery and emergencies.

Girls' Confidence

Adolescent Sexual and Reproductive Health

Running school and community-based workshops for girls aged 10-18. Topics include menstrual health management, family planning methods, self-esteem, and building resistance to gender-based violence.

Wellness and Nutrition

Community Wellness and Nutrition

Annual screening camps for chronic diseases like hypertension and diabetes, common among older women. We also conduct practical, low-cost nutrition education focused on optimizing local foods for better family health outcomes.
